Black tea

Black tea is made using the leaves of the
tea plant known as Camellia sinensis.
The leaves are oxidized, which results in
their dark brown color in the final process.

  • Has antioxidant properties
  • Protects heart health
  • Boosts energy
  • Supports mental health
  • May regulate blood sugar

Green tea

Green tea leaves come from the same plant as black tea,
but the leaves aren’t oxidized,
only dried after harvest. For this reason,
these teas are considered the
healthiest among other variations.


  • It contains only half the amount of caffeine as black tea.
  • Has fat burning properties
  • Can fight cardiovascular diseases
  • Benefits oral health
  • helps brain function

Herbal tea

Herbal teas are made by infusing fruits, roots,
herbs and stems of a variety of plants.
These teas have different benefits than regular tea,
due to the various compounds they contain.

  • They don’t contain any caffeine
  • Hibiscus and rooibos teas help prevent chronic diseases
  • Teas like peppermint, chamomile and passionflower
    can help you unwind
  • Ginseng, licorice, and cayenne tea can boost your immune system
  • Infused with different fruits they can create a wide variety of flavors
